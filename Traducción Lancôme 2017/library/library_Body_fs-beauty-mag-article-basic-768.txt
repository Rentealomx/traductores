		<h1>Clément Saccomani’s cosmopolitan Paris</h1>

	<p class="book_style_paragraph"><b>As head of Noor, a Paris-based photographic agency, 30 year-old dad Clément Saccomani has fast become known as a major talent-spotter within a changing artistic environment. His apartment, which looks out onto the rooftops of the Rue du Faubourg Saint-Denis, bares witness to his joyful, culturally-rich Parisian life. Interview.</b></p>
	<p><b>Profession:</b> Agency director at Noor <br><b>Neighbourhood:</b> 10th Arrondissement</p>
	<p><b>As a freshly minted Parisian have you adopted any typically Parisian habits?</b> I’m partly from Italy, partly from Lyon, however I grew up in the Yvelines. Since moving to Paris I’ve found myself walking everywhere. On Saturdays and Sundays I seem to be constantly walking. What I love about this city are all the things people do to make it lively and pleasant, from the way the Seine’s embankments are transformed into beaches for the summer’s <b>Paris Plages, </b>or the nostalgic <b>Guingette</b> outdoor drinking establishments like <b>Rosa Bonheur’s </b>Seine outpost<b>.<br></b><b>Le Rosa Bonheur sur Seine</b>, Quai d’Orsay. Tel. : +33 1 47 53 66 92.</p>
	<p><b>When you’re travelling abroad what do you miss most about Paris?</b> The mix of people of all colours and backgrounds that you see on the street.</p>
<div class="article_text module_title"><h2><b>If you could summarize Paris with one photo what would it be?</b> I’d say this photo doesn’t exist, I’m always looking for that same feeling of boyish wonder I remember having when my parents first took me to the Eiffel Tower just before lights-out. </h2></div>
	<p><b>If you could illustrate the feeling in Paris today with a snapshot what would it be?</b> It would be an image of France taken directly after the Charlie Hebdo massacre, or something from Martin Parr’s work in the <b>Goutte d’Or</b> neighbourhood, which wonderfully captures the diverse nature of the city.</p>
	<p><b>Describe your latest photo crush:</b> The work of young photographer <b>Samuel Gratacap</b> or that of the young artist and photographer <b>Camille Lévêque</b>, co-founder of the <b>Orpheus Standing Alone </b>publishing house. She’s just released an amazing new book titled Meng Eng Mer Sarere, (We are all mountains) which showcases Armenian cultural memory using personal family memories. The result is a polaroid and poetry-filled tome that transports readers with its showcase of traditional and contemporary visual identities.</p>
	<p><b>Name three unexpected spots that evoke Paris to you:</b> The Barbès area next to Montmartre in the 9th arrondissement; the hidden <b>Galerie Vivienne </b>covered passage in the grands boulevards area and the Louvre, especially when you look at it with childlike eyes -- at night, illuminated, by Velib bike. <br> <b>Galerie Vivienne</b>: Accessed via 6 Rue Vivienne, 4 Rue des Petits Champs or 5 Rue de la Banque, 75002 Paris.</p>
	<p><b>Name a stroll that inspires your photographic side.</b> It’s the one I do every morning by the Ourcq canal, the light there is incredible as the sun comes up.</p>
	<p><b> </b><b>Where would you live other than Paris?</b> It would either be Los Angeles, Istanbul or Marseilles. All for the light…</p>
	<p><b>What’s making you happy right now?</b> My life, my eight year-old daughter, she’s always telling me about her dreams. Her laugh seems to change every week! Little girls are really something.</p>
	<p><b>Describe a photo that makes you smile:</b> A photo of my daughter aged one. It makes me relive the time she was so small with happiness and nostalgia.</p>
	<p><b>Name three energy-boosting songs:</b> Take it easy by<b> Hopeton Lewis</b>, Hot Chip by<b> No Fit State</b>, How long by<b> Lipps Inc</b>, Dolce vita by <b>Ryan Paris</b> and Armenian music.</p>
	<p><b>How do you relax and disconnect?</b> Breathing! It’s a simple way to get some distance and to remind yourself that life is beautiful. And, by going to see the incredible chiropractor <b>Lalyr Frédéric</b> on <b>Rue Simart</b> in the 18th arrondissement.</p>
	<p><b>How do you beat the blues?</b> By flying a kite and looking after my plants.</p>
	<p><b>When did you last get the giggles?</b> On the phone with my friend. We were talking about our super powers!</p>
	<p><b>What gets you out of bed on difficult mornings?</b> I love mornings, so it’s easy!</p>
	<p><b>Do you have a specific fitness routine?</b> Climbing, or slipping on my hiking boots for a walk in the country followed up at the end of the day by a boiling hot shower followed with a blast of cold water.</p>
	<p><b>What do you do to stay in shape?</b> Jogging and biking. I have a great place I get kitted out: <b>Fixie Warehouse</b>. That and climbing. <br><b>Fixie Warehouse</b>, 2 bis Rue Bénard, 75014 Paris. Tel. : +33 6 52 43 49 03.</p>
	<p><b>If you could define Parisians by their gestures alone what would it be?</b> A little smile on the metro. Or, in fact any number of the little looks that connect Parisians throughout the day.</p>
	<p><b>Do you have a favourite barber or hairdresser? </b><b>Momo-Tif</b> a chatty and very friendly neighbourhood hairdresser on Rue de Ménlmontant, you can’t do better. Otherwise, Jean Pierre Faure in the 18th arrondissement. <br><b>Jean Pierre Faure</b>, 240 Rue Championnet, 75018 Paris. Tel. : +33 1 42 29 74 01.</p>
	<p><b>How do you envision Parisians?</b> As provincials who’ve started to hate provincials.</p>
	<p><b>What makes you feel Parisian?</b> When I’m in the car I start complaining about pedestrians and when I’m walking I complain about cars. I suppose that makes me a complainer, but one who is well-liked nonetheless. I always feel somewhat combative, a bit like<b> Rastignac</b>, one of the young and ambitious protagonists in an Honoré de Balzac novel. However, having said that I love running for buses, so…</p>
	<p><b>What’s your favourite French dish?</b> Boeuf Bourguignon because it takes hours to cook, a sign of a great meal. That and traditional sausage from Lyon, it’s my Proustian Madeleine, my childhood memory.</p>
	<p><b>What do you like to cook for your friends and family?</b> A kind of Bulghur wheat mix, with lots of things in it. It’s really good though.</p>
	<p><b>What do you eat for an energy boost?</b> Something the girl in my life makes: a Mexican drink made from tomato juice, lime, coriander, onion flowers and olive oil. No matter the time of day it’s great. It has become my go-to. Otherwise I love a good taboulé with parsley or even Ringalis, a Georgian dish.</p>
	<p><b>Clément’s Paris Address book</b><b> </b></p>
	<p><b>Where do you go to hone the eye? </b><b>Le BAL, </b>a contemporary gallery based inside a former 1920s ballroom; <b>TEMPLE</b>, a contemporary art gallery and a great place to discover emerging artists and <b>La Chambre Claire, </b>a gallery bookshop and a great place to flip through different photos from across the world. <br><b>Le BAL</b>, 6 Impasse de la Défense, 75018 Paris. Tel. : +33 1 44 70 75 50 <br><b>TEMPLE</b>, 20 Rue de la Corderie, 75003 Paris. <br><b>La Chambre Claire</b>, 14 Rue Saint-Sulpice, 75006 Paris. Tel. : +33 1 46 34 04 31.</p>
	<p><b>To decompress:</b> The Rodin museum for its architecture and its relaxing gardens. <b>La Pagode</b>, an independent cinema that’s set inside a real pagoda with a truly charming garden. Also, going to the charming <b>Prosper </b>butcher on <b>Rue Caulaincourt </b>for its exceptional meat. <br><b>Musée Rodin</b>, 79 Rue de Varenne, 75007 Paris. Tel. : +33 1 44 18 61 10. <br><b>La Pagode</b>, 57 bis Rue de Babylone, 75007 Paris. Tel. : +33 1 45 55 48 48. <br><b>Boucherie Prosper,</b> 61 Rue de Caulaincourt, 75018 Paris.</p>
	<p><b>For coffee or a cocktail:</b> I love to just go for a coffee in the <b>Luxembourg gardens</b> or in the gardens of the <b>Palais-Royal</b>. In the evening, I swear by <b>L’Arsouille </b>bar for its Montmartre neighborhood vibe. Or, <b>Rosebud </b>which does some of the best craft cocktails in town.<br><b>Jardin du Luxembourg</b>, 75006 Paris. <br><b>Jardin du Palais-Royal</b>, 2 Place Colette, 75001 Paris. <br><b>L’Arsouille</b>, 22 Rue des Trois Frères, 75018 Paris. Tel. : +33 1 42 55 53 37. <br><b>Le Rosebud</b>, 11 B Rue Delambre, 75014 Paris. Tel. : +33 1 43 35 38 54.</p>
	<p><b>For dinners with friends:</b> My apartment! Or, more seriously I really feel at home at the Armenian Cultural house, <b>Maison de la Culture Arménienne</b> and very welcomed by the owner of <b>La Galère des Rois.</b> I have a soft spot for Japanese food from <b>Guilo Guilo</b>, its chef spends odd months in <b>Kyoto</b> and even months in <b>Paris</b>, meaning that the dishes are always inspired by the latest trends from Japan.</p>
	<p><b>For picking up home design goods:</b><b>Saint-Ouen </b>flea market. I also go abroad to London or Brussels, for their antiques districts. <br><b>Marché aux puces de Saint Ouen</b>, 30 Avenue Gabriel Péri, 93400 Saint-Ouen.</p>
	<p><b>Photos</b> : Charlotte Abramow</p>
